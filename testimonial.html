- Lisa Marie (recent immigrant to Canada (7 years)

The Paul Atanya I have known for the past 7 years is not the same Paul Atanya I now know. After reading your book, DESTINATION CANADA: Tools for Success, I will no longer talk to you like I used to talk to Paul Atanya. I did not know. I will start talking to you as the real Paul—the Paul Atanya I now know. I will be taking you seriously. I feel guilty because I didn't buy your book, even when you suggested I buy and read your book. I remember telling you that I did not only 'have the time to read' but that 'I had not the time for myself'. What a pity!

Fortunately, I picked up a copy of your book from Roy's residence and read the introduction and the first chapter but couldn't stop. So, I took the book from his house and brought it to my house. What you have written about in your book highly resonates with me. And do you know why? If I had known who you are and had asked you about what I should do to advance my career in Canada, I would have obtained my bachelor's and master's degrees, or I would even be pursuing my PhD or working in an office. Here I am, working in a warehouse! I did not try to seek advice. I took you for granted.

I just kept calling you to find out if you were free so I could drop by for a coffee, and you always said “yes”. Now, I realise that if I had known what a Career and Employment Consultant was all about, I would have advanced my career to the next level, found an office job, and not continued to work in a warehouse. That is not to say that a warehouse job is necessarily bad.

Thank you, Paul, for making me see the light. Thank you for opening my eyes. I’m going to recommend your book to my friends. I won’t give them the current copy I’m reading to any of my friends for free, like I did. I will ask them to buy it.
